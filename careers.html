Agriculture Scientists

Median Pay: $58,380-$68,830

Education: Bachelor's degree, Masters recommended

Employment Change: (2020-2030) 9%

Recommended Courses: Biology, Chemistry, Math

Agriculture Scientists look at different types of solutions and ideas to produce more crop yield, though this may not always be economically friendly, it is getting better. Often these can help quite a bit in lowering the amount of unnecessary inorganic fertilizers that would usually be used. Many of these solutions are listed below.

Adopting Nutrient Management Techniques

By controlling the amount, method, and placement of the fertilizer or manure being used, the effectiveness can be significantly improved.

Conservation Drainage Practices

As explained before, nitrogen and phosphorus often get swept away with water into nearby aquatic ecosytems, potentially endangering them. The impacts can be minimized by modifying drainage systems to be the best they can be, you can minimize the impacts.

Implementing Conservation Tillage

Reducing how often and how intensely farmers till their land could improve soil health, prevent erosion, runoff, soil compaction, etc. and therefore lessons the chance of nutrients being lost.

Ensuring Year-Round Ground Cover

During the winter time, covering up soil and crops can easily ensure less erosion and prevent loss of nutrients.

Planting Field Buffers

Planting trees and shrubs can easily help filter out things like nitrogen and phosphorus from water, making it much more safe to go into aquatic habitats.

Managing Livestock Access to Streams

By installing fences, farmers and ranchers can stop animals and livestock from drinking or going to a certain location along a lake or river. This in turn can prevent excess nutrients from entering the water.
